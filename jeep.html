Toyota (Jeep FJ40)

Back in the 1950's the American Army contacted the Toyota Motor Corporation with a request for a 4WD vehicle to be used by the soldiers and military police stationed in Japan. Toyota took the request seriously.

The result: the Land Cruiser, a literally bulletproof vehicle. Down to earth yet with spirited attitude, capable of enlivening the life you're living and the conversation you're having. All FJ40 Land Cruisers

Toyota (4Runner)

The Toyota 4Runner isn't getting any younger—this generation originally went on sale in 2010, making it officially a teenager and one of the oldest vehicles on sale in America but it is getting some new goodies for the 2024 model year, even if a new generation is expected soon.

We also have official pricing. And most exciting of all, a new TRD Pro exclusive paint color, seen above: Terra.

Toyota (Land Cruiser 2024)

Built in Japan at the Tahara and Hino plants on the same fully-boxed frame that underpins the new Tundra, as well as the 2024 Tacoma and Lexus GX, the new Land Cruiser comes to our shores in three grades: A base 1958 version with round headlights reminiscent of the FJ40 and FJ60; a Land Cruiser edition with square headlamps giving a nod to the FJ62; and a First Edition with round headlamps limited to 5,000 units in North America, about 300 of which are destined for Canada. The first 5,000 units off the assembly line will all be First Edition units.

Toyota( Sequoia 2023)

The Sequoia comes with the iForce MAX hybrid system by default, unlike the Tundra which has a nonhybrid twin-turbocharged 3.4-liter V6 engine. Between the twin-turbo V6 and the 10-speed automatic transmission is an electric motor powered by a 1.5 kWh nickel-metal-hydride battery pack that combines with the V6 to provide an impressive output of 583 lb-ft of torque and 437 hp.

Kia (Sportage)

The 2023 Kia Sportage impresses with excellent cargo space, a high-tech and well-crafted interior and a balanced ride and handling. However, it falls short in terms of engine performance, and many common tech and safety features are only available in higher trims.
